Journey to Software Development in Calgary


Born and raised in Ontario, I moved to Calgary to study at the University of Calgary and run for the DINOS Cross-Country team.

As an athlete, I was fortunate to win the 2016 Canada West Cross-Country Rookie of the Year award and become a member of the team that went on to win the 2018 and 2019 USPORTS National Championships.

As a student, I was fortunate to take my first ever programming course, ENGG 233 - Computing for Engineers, in my first year, and realize just how exciting working with computers can be!

I then decided to pursue Software Engineering specifically, and have spent the last few years soaking up as much knowledge as possible. During the summer of 2019, I had my first work experience in software development at the Calgary Counselling Centre, and in the summer of 2020 I will begin a 16-month internship working on software at Lockheed Martin CDL Systems. I have also had the opportunity to work with a friend on a real-world project for a school cafeteria in his hometown of Darien, Illnois.

It is exciting to have reached the point where my work is out interacting with people in the real world, and thinking about it pushes me to make sure I am working hard to deliver the best possible software to the user.
