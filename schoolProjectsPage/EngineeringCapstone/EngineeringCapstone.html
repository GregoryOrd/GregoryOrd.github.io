Engineering Capstone Project (Jan-Mar 2022)

Project Description

As part of the engineering ciriculum at every Canadian university, students post complete a group capstone project in their final year. The capstone projects are sponsored by companies, and the company that my group worked with was a startup company hoping to use our work in their development of a penetrating radar (GPR) system. The company had an antenna some some electronics to control the antenna but needed to develop their signal processing algorithm to process the antenna data.

Our group of four students divided into pairs that this project. The first pair worked on improving things on the electronics and hardware side, while the second pair (which I was part of) worked on the software side. For the software, we needed to create a signal processing algorithm that could take the raw radar data and process it to create plots and determine the location of objects. The other member of my pair had a signal processing background and did most of the work designing the algorithm, while I implemented it in python.

To take a "scan", the antenna was moved along a one dimensional line with measurements taken at regular intervals along the line. At each measurement location, the antenna would send out a radar signal and measure the reflected signal that came back to the antenna. For each of these locations we generated plots to visualize the raw signal in both the frequency and time domains.

Some filtering and data processing was applied to each of these location plots, and then at the end we created a heat map that combined the data from all of the individual location measurements to show reflected signal strength at each location along the line with various two-way travel times. The two-way travel time is porportional to the depth of the object, so this heat map allowed us to visualize the location of any objects that existed in the scan.

We never tested in a real-world scenario, but by the end of the project we were able to create a solution that worked in a lab test to detect the depth and location of a pip buried in a sand box. In the first image above (the heatmap), you can see the pipe at around location 20 on the x-axis and around a two-way travel time of 40ns on the y-axis.
