In 2016, I moved to Calgary to study at the University of Calgary and run for the university's Cross-Country team. During my time there I was fortunate to win the 2016 Canada West Cross-Country Rookie of the Year award and be surrounded by great teammates. Together our team was able to win silver at the 2017 National Championships, and gold at the 2018 and 2019 editions.

It was also at the University of Calgary where I had my first programming experience: a first year course, ENGG 233 - Computing for Engineers. This first experience led me to choose Software Engineering as my specialization that the end of the first year of general engineering.

Now 10 years later, I have 5+ years of work experience and I am still grateful for the exposure I received during that first year course.

Currently, my work is primarily in C++, Java, and Python on Linux, but also have professional experience with Windows, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and C#.

Outside of work I am still involved in my local running community (training, racing, coaching, and volunteering), alongside working on personal projects and doing a little bit of language study (Spanish).

Curious and collaborative, I enjoy learning new technologies and building practical solutions. Explore my work experience and personal projects on this site to learn more.
